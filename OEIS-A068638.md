# OEIS A068638

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) denote the set of composite positive integers.

Define the sequence \((a_n)_{n \ge 1}\) by
\[
a_1 = 1,
\]
and for each \(n \ge 2\),
\[
a_n = \min \Bigl\{\, m \in \mathcal{C} \setminus \{a_2,a_3,\dots,a_{n-1}\} \;:\; m + a_k \in \mathcal{C}\ \text{for all}\ 1 \le k \le n-1 \Bigr\}.
\]

Equivalently, \(a_n\) is the smallest **new** composite integer such that
\[
a_n + a_k
\]
is composite for every earlier term \(a_k\), and also \(a_n + a_n = 2a_n\) is composite.

The sequence begins
\[
1,\,8,\,14,\,20,\,24,\,25,\,26,\,32,\,38,\,44,\dots
\]

OEIS description:

> \(a(1)=1\), and for \(n \ge 2\), \(a(n)\) is the smallest distinct composite number such that \(a(n)+a(k)\) is composite for all \(k=1,\dots,n\).

A stated OEIS comment is:

> Conjecture: 25 is the largest odd term of this sequence.
